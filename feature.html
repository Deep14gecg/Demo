Features



Custom Dashboard

Your Dashboard. Your Design.

Take full control of your project by creating a custom dashboard with drag-and-drop widgets to visualize, manage, and control connected devices. Add and remotely control sensors, actuators, and more to single board computers, microcontrollers, and other hardware connected to Cayenne.

Alerts

Be in the Know

Cayenne has a built in a rules engine to easily set up email and text alerts when a device or sensor reaches a predetermined state. With Cayenne fully integrated rules engine all you have to do is drag-and-drop devices into the Triggers & Alerts section to create threshold notifications to a single recipient or a group.

Remot Control

Access Projects from Anywhere

The Cayenne phone app is available for Android and iPhone so you can monitor and control your dashboard and projects on the go. Dashboard widgets enables you to control your project with just a few taps. Easily toggle a light on/off with a button widget, adjust the temperature of a thermostat with a knob widget, you can also use a slider widget to adjust a light dimmer, and more.

Scheduling

Schedule Automation

Hardware connected to Cayenne benefits from an integrated scheduling feature to easily and quickly program time-based events. The event screen provides a simplified approach to creating schedules. Name the event, date and time of occurrence, and the frequency. You can also setup an alert for a post-event notification. You can also filter by device.

Triggers

Create Triggers in a Snap

As soon as you connect your first device to your project, Cayenne empowers builders to create automated IoT projects in minutes. Device data captured in real-time enables the execution of triggered events. This allows for seamless device-to-device communication regardless of manufacturer. Code Customization

Complete Flexibility

Easily integrate your devices that use custom code, viewing and controlling them using the same great dashboard and mobile app support that Cayenne has for native devices. Writing custom code for devices connected to an Arduino is accomplished using sketch files. By writing your code in the sketch file, you have complete control over how your data is sent to Cayenne.
